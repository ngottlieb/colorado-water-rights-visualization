Overview

High level water distribution amongst the seven states in the Colorado Basin and Mexico is governed by a series of laws and treaties passed over the last century. These laws were built upon a faulty assumption that the mean annual flow at Lee's Ferry (the dividing point between Upper and Lower Basins) was 16.4 million acre feet (maf). It is now understood to be significantly lower, and it is becoming clearer that our existing legal system for distributing water is not sufficient in the face of increasing demand and potentially decreasing supply.
